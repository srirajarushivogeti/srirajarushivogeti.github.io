264 Raja dan Lembaga Adat Ikuti Festival Adat Budaya Nusantara II di Jateng 264 Kings and Customary Institutions Join the 2nd Nusantara Cultural Festival in Central Java

20 Desember 2022 20 December 2022

← Kembali ke Berita ← Back to News

Sebanyak 264 raja, lembaga adat, dan suku se-Nusantara yang tergabung dalam MATRA menghadiri Festival Adat Budaya Nusantara II di kawasan Candi Borobudur, Magelang. Gubernur Jawa Tengah Ganjar Pranowo membuka acara. 264 kings, customary institutions, and tribes of the archipelago united in MATRA attended the 2nd Nusantara Cultural Festival near Candi Borobudur, Magelang. Central Java Governor Ganjar Pranowo opened the event.

MetroTV — Sebanyak 264 raja, lembaga adat, dan suku seluruh Nusantara yang tergabung dalam Masyarakat Adat Nusantara (MATRA) menghadiri acara Festival Adat Budaya Nusantara ke-II di kawasan Candi Borobudur, Magelang, Jawa Tengah, Jumat (9/12/2022).

Gubernur Jawa Tengah Ganjar Pranowo membuka acara tersebut. Ia mengatakan acara ini digelar sebagai upaya memperkuat silaturahmi kerajaan dan lembaga adat sekaligus menunjukkan kepedulian pada ketahanan adat dan budaya Nusantara.

Dalam acara itu, Ganjar menyaksikan MATRA mendeklarasikan komitmen untuk melestarikan dan menjaga ketahanan adat serta budaya nasional.

Sebelumnya, Festival Adat Budaya Nusantara pertama kali diadakan di Bali, dan Festival Adat Budaya Nusantara ke-III direncanakan dilaksanakan di Sumatera Barat.

(Telah dimuat di Metro TV)

MetroTV — A total of 264 kings, customary institutions, and tribes from across the archipelago united in Masyarakat Adat Nusantara (MATRA) attended the 2nd Nusantara Cultural Festival near Candi Borobudur, Magelang, Central Java, on Friday (9/12/2022).

Central Java Governor Ganjar Pranowo opened the event. He said it was held to strengthen the bonds between kingdoms and customary institutions while demonstrating care for the resilience of the archipelago’s customs and culture.

During the event, Ganjar witnessed MATRA declare its commitment to preserving and safeguarding national customary and cultural resilience.

The Nusantara Cultural Festival was first held in Bali, and the third edition is planned to take place in West Sumatra.

(As published on Metro TV)
